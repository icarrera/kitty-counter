Note: We're not linking to Grumpy Cat, because, really, what on earth does she have to be grumpy about? She gets to travel the world, and on top of that she's getting free Friskies for life. Cry me a river about how much angst being a star gives you. Plus, one of our team sat through that awful Christmas movie from beginning to end, and there's no way to get those two hours of life back. Here are some much less grumpy kitty sites for you to check out:

Links

Cat Bounce

We're not quite sure why this site is so addicting, but addicting it is. It's exactly as described: click and drag the kitties, and they'll bounce off the bottom of the window. There's even a place you can click to "make it rain" cats (and no dogs...)

Cats on Know Your Meme

A short history of cats on the Internet. If you have no idea what a LOLCat is, this would be a good place to start. (If you don't even know what a cat is, that first sentence will explain it all for you.)

LOLCats on I Can Has Cheezburger

Though the site as a whole lately has been devoting excessive amounts of attention to (ugh) dogs, they're still the main go-to place where oodles of cat memes have been and are being created and distributed.

Men and Cats

The antidote to the crazy-cat-lady stereotype, this is a blog featuring photos and videos of men with their feline companions.

Seattle Meowtropolitan: Seatte's Cat Cafe

Did I just write 'cafes'? Right now there's only one cat cafe in the area. Seattle Meowtropolitan has a maximum capacity of 15 patrons (so as not to overstimulate their residents), so one must make reservations to spend a 50-minute hour with their kitties.
